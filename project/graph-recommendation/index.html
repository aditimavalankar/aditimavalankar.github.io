Advanced Bayesian Methods for Graph-based Recommendations

 

This project (done by a team of 3 members) extends the work done by Lopes et al. in their paper ‘Efficient Bayesian Methods for Graph-based Recommendation’. The authors suggest 3-step paths from the target user to each candidate item, and then use probabilistic methods to rank all these candidate items for recommendation.

We incorporate the basic idea of quantifying similarity between users and items into the existing ranking methods that the original paper uses. Our results show an improvement over the performance of the original paper in all 4 metrics that the authors have used. We perform ablation experiments to see which similarity/similarities will result in the highest improvement over the baseline performance. We also use a user reliability score, which is a global measure for every user, but that leads to a decrease in performance as it is highly generalized.

We repeat the same set of experiments for 2 datasets, in order to establish the generic nature of our extensions. Our baseline performance matches that of the original paper, which validates our implementation. The results obtained for both these datasets are comparable, and this shows the robustness of our added measures of user-user similarity and item-item similarity.
